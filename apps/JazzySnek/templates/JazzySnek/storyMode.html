It's full black out... well past the time for more sensible critters to slip into their lily whites. The smell of cigarette smoke hangs in the air.

It's the scent of run-down dive bars and work weeks that've stretched on for far too long...

...the cologne of tired working class stiffs and manic-depressive artists. It's the lingering smell of shady alleys meshed with urban sophistication.

And that's what I'm here for. Not the stink of cigarette smoke.

No, I'm here for the music.

I wait patiently in line with the other jazz-heads. They shift their weight and check their watches, trying to endure the tedium that'd stretched them around the corner not too long ago.

But each mincing little step forward is a wave that percolates down the line, and watching the movement of all those bodies in offset synchrony... well, that's just a taste of what's to come.

I'm so close now. The doors to the club swing open, and a gust of hot air hits me in the face.

The sharp trill of a singer cuts through the otherwise muted night, and the rhythm of the music, like the heartbeat of the nightclub, resonates through the concrete and up my body.

I slither up to the bouncer. Presenting ol' Saint Peter with my ID, I straighten up and try to look like I belong.

“Forty bucks to get in,” he says in a bass rumble. The guy is built like a gorilla, and he sounds like one to boot.

“Here you go.”

I pass him a couple Andrew Jacksons. I can hardly spare the Presidents, but here's hoping it'll be worth it.

I'm about to enter the club proper when a figure bars my way.

“Hey! What are you doing here!?”

“Huh?” I blink. The angry arachnid who's just stopped me reeks of alcohol. “I'm just going in. I love swing music.”

“You don't belong here, Jack. You're a snek.”

“My name isn't Jack. I'm JazzySnek.”

“Like hell you are! You've got no groove, Sonny-Jim. Sneks can't swing dance!”

I look to the bouncer to intervene, but he isn't saying anything. In fact, he's making a point to ignore this altercation and focus on the other clubbers. This fella must be one of the regulars.

“Who says sneks can't swing dance?”

“Sneks don't wear hats. And having a hat is central to being a proper swing dancer. Now hit the curb, ya dewdapper. Or you want me to go big six on yous?”

I refrain from pointing out that I do, indeed, have a hat. A fine, spectacular fedora in fact.

I'm not about to leave just because some drunk spider doesn't like the shape of my head.

Problem though is that if the bouncer isn't stepping in between us, this fella here must be a real jitter bug... one with enough clout to act like he owns the place and get away with it.

In this world holding your own on the dance floor is everything, and by the look of this fella he's got a mean set of stilts.

I'm stuck. I can't get in with this hopped up high-hat holding the gate, and I doubt the bouncer is just gonna give me back my money.

But before things escalate, another figure steps out of the club and intervenes.

“Hold on now! What's going on here?”

The newcomer examines me through his monocle, adjusting his top hat as he leans in.

The Foptopus! I can hardly believe my eyes. One of the best swing dancers back in the day, and a real swell fella to boot, if his reputation is accurate.

Half dapper gent, half hep cat, half octopus. And he's interceding on my behalf!

Or so I hope.

“SpideyLegs! Why are you harassing this patron?”

“Just keepin' the riffraff out. The regulars don't come for a clambake, and this newcomer is green to the gills. Literally!”

“Sneks don't have gills.”

“Well! I would've thought you were better than that, SpideyLegs! You know that isn't how we do things around here.”

“Fine! We'll settle things on the dancefloor then! Frisk your whiskers and set the tune. It's time to DANCE!”
